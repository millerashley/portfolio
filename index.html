Benchtop Setup for ARF-OCE Imaging

This project involved designing a custom waterbath fixture to align ultrasound and OCT imaging systems in a lab-based tissue-mimicking setup. The goal was to improve the effective pushing resolution of ARF by integrating a new transducer and aligning it with the OCT beam.

  • Designed and fabricated a custom waterbath fixture to align optical and acoustic paths for ARF-OCE imaging
  • Enabled improved resolution of the acoustic radiation force (ARF) focal spot using a newly mounted transducer
  • Iterated CAD models and tested mechanical alignments to integrate OCT and ultrasound transducers in a tissue-mimicking setup
  • Presented findings in two lab meetings and submitted a final semester research report

This project collection explores the cardiovascular system through fluid dynamics simulation, numerical transport modeling, and developmental biomechanics analysis.

In a computational fluid dynamics (CFD) study using Ansys Fluent, I simulated blood flow through a bifurcating carotid artery with and without obstruction. Using realistic CAD geometries and mesh refinement strategies, I analyzed how arterial narrowing affects flow behavior â€” specifically, increases in wall shear stress and flow recirculation zones that can contribute to conditions like aneurysms. The results demonstrated the role of CFD in assessing cardiovascular health risks and optimizing treatment designs.

In a separate biomechanics research project, I authored a review paper on cardiac looping morphogenesis â€” the earliest symmetry-breaking event in heart development. I compared two major modeling approaches: an integrated finite element model based on experimental observations (Shi et al., 2014), and a geometric instability model driven by residual stress (Bevilacqua et al., 2021). This work deepened my understanding of how mechanical forces and material properties drive tissue morphogenesis and shape organ development.

Additionally, I explored 1D and 2D transport phenomena using Python-based numerical methods to simulate diffusion and advection in blood vessels. While an exploratory project, it helped solidify my understanding of discretization schemes and the interplay between transport mechanics and boundary conditions â€” foundational for modeling drug delivery and solute transport in biomedical contexts.

Future Concept: POTS Water Bottle

My most recent personal engineering project is a smart water bottle designed to support individuals with POTS (Postural Orthostatic Tachycardia Syndrome), a chronic condition involving autonomic nervous system dysfunction that causes symptoms like fatigue, dizziness, and brain fog. Because increasing water and sodium intake is a cornerstone of POTS management, I'm developing an early-stage concept for a smart bottle that integrates electrolyte dosing, medication storage, and biofeedback features. The design includes a built-in hydration tracker and pulse oximeter for real-time heart rate monitoring. As someone with POTS myself, my goal is to create practical assistive technology that helps users manage symptoms and regain autonomy in daily life. Check back for more developments later!
